About

I received Bachelor of Arts degrees in Economics and Public Policy from The University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, and I graduated with Highest Distinction. I am currently a Graduate Student in the Educational Leadership, Policy, and Organization PhD Program at Vanderbilt University, and I am studying K-12 education policy. Through this program, I have gained experience with quantitative research using large scale datasets, statistical and regression analysis and qualitative research analyzing data from videos and interviews.

I am specifically interested in researching informal education spaces and their role in the lives of Black youth. I am currently working on three research projects as a graduate research assistant: (1) the informal education spaces, (2) socioeconomic status and differences in the socialization of children, and (3) a meta-analysis about Black girls in STEM.
